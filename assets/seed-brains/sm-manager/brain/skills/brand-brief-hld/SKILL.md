# Hand Lane Designs — Brand Brief (store)

This is the HLD side of the social team. It is a **pre-filled brand brief** — do
NOT run the generic 6-question interview for HLD; the answers are below. When you
start an HLD job, write this content to `brand-brief.md` in the working directory
(so `post-writer` and `post-grader` read it natively), then proceed.

Source of truth (refresh from here if they change):
`~/Code/hld-admin/.claude/skills/brand-voice` and `product-facts`, and vault memory
`user_hld-brand-facts`.

## The brief (maps to the standard brand-brief.md fields)

**1. Business — what we sell**
Custom laser engraving, run by Ryan Naquin with his wife Meredith. Tumblers, cutting
boards, drinkware, signs, and gifts, each one hand-engraved to order on a Trotec
Speedy 360. Sold on Shopify and Etsy. Based in **New Braunfels, TX** (NEVER "Round
Rock").

**2. Customer — one real person**
Someone buying a GIFT for a person they care about, who wants it to feel personal and
last. Dads, grads, coaches, wedding parties, "just because." They'd rather buy from a
real Texas maker than a drop-shipper.

**3. One action after seeing a post**
Visit the store (or DM for a custom order). One CTA per post. Gift-framing beats
"what do you think?"

**4. Point of view (HLD's warm wedge — NOT a polarizing hot-take)**
Engraving should be permanent — laser-etched into the material, never a vinyl sticker
that peels or a print that fades. A one-of-a-kind gift made by a real person beats
mass-produced. This conviction is warm and proud, not contrarian bait. HLD virality
is OFF: never growth-hacker energy.

**5. Recent story / raw material**
Pull from the actual product or order at hand (a design someone ordered, a gift moment
a customer described). If none is given, use the most common customer question as the
angle. Never invent a story.

**6. Voice — warm Texas maker, not a marketing department**
- Sound like Ryan talking to a customer at a craft fair: warm, direct, proud of the
  work, zero corporate gloss.
- Concrete over hype. Say what it is, what it's made of, who it's for. Let the product
  carry the excitement.
- The craft term is **"hand-engraved."** Permanence is a selling point (never a
  sticker, never fades; engraved powder-coat is hand-wash, not dishwasher-safe).
- A little folksy is on-brand ("holds ice for days," "built for the deer lease or the
  office"). Playful, specific title flair is good ("...for Dads on the Go!").
- Buyer skims on a phone: short sentences, short paragraphs.

## Hard rules (a post that breaks ANY of these is rejected by post-grader)

- **Banned clichés:** "elevate," "look no further," "premium quality" (unqualified),
  "perfect for any occasion," "makes a statement," "sleek and stylish," "crafted with
  care," em-dash-heavy AI cadence, three adjectives in a row.
- **Location:** New Braunfels, TX. Never Round Rock.
- **Craft term:** "hand-engraved."
- **Product facts (guardrail — from `product-facts`):** dimensions, capacity,
  materials, insulation, care, and compatibility claims may come ONLY from the job
  payload or Hand Lane's existing catalog data. Never fabricate hour-ratings (say
  "double-wall vacuum insulated," not "keeps drinks cold 24 hours"), dishwasher-safe
  claims (engraved powder-coat is hand-wash), brand names (only if the product name
  includes it, e.g. YETI/Polar Camel), social proof (no invented reviews/bestseller
  badges), or licensing/affiliation with schools, teams, or the military. When unsure,
  omit the claim — a shorter honest post beats an invented one.
- **Shared copy rules:** contractions, digits not words ("3 tips"), NO em-dashes,
  active voice, one concrete idea per post.
